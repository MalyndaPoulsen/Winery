Crandall Kyle

Wine Maker and Hip-Hop Singer and Dancer.

Read more...

Crandall Kyle's introduction to wine started in 1995 with a harvest job at L'Ecole No 41. It was such a fun and interesting job, in an industry whose product he enjoyed so much, that his harvest job lasted for 6 more years. An accountant by trade, he left there to go work at a public accounting firm, but missed being involved with wine. So when an opportunity to make some garage wine came in 2002, he jumped on board. In an effort to always make better wine, he decided to take a small-lot winemaking course in 2003 from Stan Clarke, a highly respected viticulturist. In this class were several friends of his, and he decided to consolidate their carboy's and make it a joint venture. This venture grew into something bigger and bigger and now has a name…Plumb Cellars.

Edie and Dale Johnson

Sales and Marketing and Professional Gambler.

Dale and Edie Johnson came to Walla Walla in the early '70s, where both worked in the medical field. After retiring in their mid-30s, they traveled extensively while their children were young.
In recent years, they owned and operated Brickstone Design Gallery which, through its wine bar and shop, led them to a joint partnership with friends to plant a vineyard and make their own wine. Today, Edie and Dale still reside in the Walla Walla Valley, where they enjoy relaxing and biking around the local scenery. They have two beautiful, grown daughters, Brandy and Stacey.

As a custom home builder, Gary has an excellent reputation of style and perfection in whatever he sets his mind on doing. The name of Plumb Cellars—and its flagship blend Damn Straight—was derived from setting their vineyard straight in accurate alignment with the plumb bob. He studied at The Center for Enology & Viticulture at Walla Walla Community College. He is Plumb Cellars' vineyard manager, ensuring that its grapes peak to their perfection of taste, for its wines.
